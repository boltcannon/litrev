ET606 Research Tools: Comparative Analysis

Educational Technology, IIT Bombay

The Assignment Task

This project involved a comprehensive review and hands-on evaluation of various digital tools designed to streamline the academic literature review process. The goal was to understand how a researcher navigates the entire research workflow—from initial discovery to final synthesis—using a mix of traditional, AI-powered, and visual platforms.

Core Research Question: "How Learning Analytics and Educational Data Mining are implemented in MOOCs."

5 Key Stages of the Literature Review

1

Searching & Discovery

Finding the foundational and current papers. Tools: Google Scholar, Consensus.

2

Organizing & Managing

Structuring the collected sources and references. Tools: SCISPACE, Novix.

3

Visualizing Connections

Mapping citation networks and trends. Tools: Research Rabbit, Connected Papers. [Image of a research paper citation network diagram]

4

Analyzing & Synthesizing

Extracting, comparing, and summarizing findings. Tools: Novix, SCISPACE, Elicit.

5

Writing & Presentation

Drafting the report and creating interactive outputs. Tools: SCISPACE, H5P.

Comparative Analysis of Research Tools

Tool Type Strengths Limitations Best-fit Stage
Google Scholar Traditional Comprehensive coverage, simple, free, prioritizes highly-cited papers. No synthesis or visualization features, limited advanced search filters. Searching
SCISPACE AI-based Deep synthesis, structured reports, references mapped, AI Copilot for paper interaction. May require premium access for full features (e.g., unlimited literature reviews). Analysis & Writing
Novix AI-based Phased workflow, detailed report generation, good for organization. Limited transparency, comparison features are less granular. Organization & Writing
Consensus AI-based Quick summaries, citation metrics, excellent for quick relevance check. Short summaries, shallow depth of analysis. Searching
Research Rabbit Visual Interactive citation graphs, fast and exhaustive discovery, collection-based recommendations. Needs an initial input paper, can be overwhelming with results. Visualization
Connected Papers Visual Clearly maps citation lineage (Prior/Derivative Works), very simple UI, fast loading. Requires a single seed paper, less overall functionality compared to competitors. Visualization
Elicit AI Assistant Simplified summaries, organizes findings into customizable synthesis tables. Surface-level understanding, may lack the critical depth of full analysis. Analysis
H5P Interactive Excellent for creating interactive presentations and surveys, good for knowledge check. Not designed for literature analysis or search itself. Presentation

Conclusion: The Recommended Hybrid Pipeline

The most effective literature review relies on a Hybrid Approach, leveraging each tool for its specific strength in the workflow.

Effective Pipeline Flow:

1. Google Scholar + Consensus → 2. Research Rabbit + Connected Papers → 3. SCISPACE + Novix
  • Quick Find: Google Scholar and Consensus for initial high-level search.
  • Network Mapping: Research Rabbit to visualize how papers and authors are interconnected.
  • Deep Dive: SCISPACE and Novix for analysis, content extraction, and report drafting.
  • Support Tools: Elicit for simplifying difficult papers, and H5P for interactive result presentation.
